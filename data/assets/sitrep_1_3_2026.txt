Geopolitical Dissolution and Regime Transition: A Comprehensive Analysis of the Venezuelan Crisis and United States Military Intervention of January 2026The structural integrity of the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela underwent a fundamental and violent transformation in the early hours of January 3, 2026. The execution of a large-scale United States military operation, culminating in the capture and extraction of President Nicolás Maduro and First Lady Cilia Flores, represents the most significant shift in South American geopolitics since the end of the Cold War.1 This intervention, following years of escalating internal repression and months of targeted American maritime and economic pressure, has effectively decapitated the executive branch of the Venezuelan state.3 The situation as of today reflects a nation at a crossroads between systemic reconstruction under a democratic mandate and a potential descent into civil strife characterized by "warlordization," as remnants of the previous regime and the resurgent democratic opposition compete for control over the country’s vast natural resources and institutional levers.5The transition from a state of "maximum pressure" to direct kinetic intervention was announced by United States President Donald Trump through social media channels at approximately 4:30 a.m. EST, confirming that American forces had conducted a "brilliant" and "large-scale" strike against the Venezuelan leadership.6 The capture of Maduro, who has been flown to a U.S. warship and is currently en route to face narco-terrorism charges in New York, has triggered a nationwide state of emergency in Venezuela and an immediate emergency session of the United Nations Security Council.8 The following report provides a detailed examination of the operational execution, the economic and energy-sector collapse that precipitated the strike, the humanitarian landscape,
